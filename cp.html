What is CP?

Competitive programming is a programming competition generally involving the host presenting a set of logical or mathematical problems to the contestants and contestants are required to write computer programs capable of solving each problem. Judging is based mostly upon number of problems solved and time spent for writing successful solutions, but may also include other factors (quality of output produced, execution time, memory usage, program size, etc.).

Platfroms for Competitive Programming

Codeforces

This is a platform where you can get a real taste of what CP is like. The contests are more challenging than those on CodeChef and the time limit of 2 hours is also less. As a beginner, this platform is not recommended as you may lose interest in CP if you are unable to solve problems (which is highly likely). When you want to get started, try giving Div 3 contests, then move on to Div 2.

Codechef

A CodingChef contest is a suitable place to begin your CP journey since the starting questions are easy to solve and the difficulty level gradually increases. The time limit of three hours is sufficient to solve the problems. During the contest, you can also see a live rating graph that can raise or lower your morale based on your performance.

Leetcode

One of the best places to get prepared for DSA-based interviews. This is because the time limit of the contest is similar to what companies give and the questions are also fully DSA-based. Don't start giving contests before you have completed all the basic data structures. If you don't understand even the basics of the data structure used in the question, you won't be able to solve it.

Practicing questions on these platforms is essential for excelling in these coding contests since you will get a sense of the types of questions and you will be able to solve them, which will increase your overall problem-solving abilities.
